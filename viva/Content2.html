Healthy Eating

  • Add more fruit and vegetables to your diet:

    Fruits and vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, fibre and low in calories. Professionals in healthcare have recommended to get minimum of five servings of fruit and vegetables daily. It will naturally satisfy your hunger and reduce your intake of bad foods.

  • Plan your meals on higher fibre:

    Your best fiber choices are Whole-grain products, Vegetables and fruits, Beans, peas, Nuts and seeds and other legumes. They can provide health benefits such as helping to maintain a healthy weight, lowering your risk of diabetes, cholesterol, heart diseases and some type of cancer and helps you live longer.

  • Limit added sugars:

    Reduce excessive added sugars, including cane juice, corn syrup, dextrose, fructose, table sugar, maple syrup, honey, and sugary drinks, can lead to weight gain, obesity, type 2 diabetes, and heart disease.

    Limit added sugars by drinking water, adding fruits to cereal or yogurt, not stocking up on sugary drinks and snacks, asking for low-fat milk or unsweetened soy beverages at coffee shops, and reading nutrition labels to choose foods with lower sugar amounts.

  • Stay Hydrated:

    To prevent dehydration, you need to drink plenty of fluids. The government recommends drinking 6 to 8 glasses daily. Water, lower fat milk,lower sugar drinks including tea and coffee, all non-alcoholic drinks are helthier choices. You must drink more fluids during hot weather or while exercising.

  • Do not skip breakfast:

    Some people believe skipping breakfast may result in weight loss. However, a nutritious breakfast that is low in fat, sugar, and salt and high in fibre can be a component of a balanced diet and help you obtain the nutrients you require for optimal health.

  • Eat oily fish:

    Fish is a protein-rich source with numerous vitamins and minerals. Consume at least 2 portions a week, including oily fish, which are high in omega-3 fats and may prevent heart disease. Oily fish include Salmon, sardines, mackerel etc.
