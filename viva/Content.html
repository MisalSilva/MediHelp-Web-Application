United Nation's SDGs

The Sustainable Development Goals are a bold, universal agreement to end poverty in all it's dimentions and craft an equal, just and secure world - for people, planet and prosperity by 2030.

GOOD HEALTH AND WELL-BEING

Why Good Health and Well-Being is important as a Planet ?

Good health and well-being are vital for the planet's sustainability and prosperity.
They ensure a thriving population capable of contributing positively to society, economic growth, and environmental conservation.
Prioritizing health fosters resilience and fosters a harmonious relationship between humanity and the planet.

Below are some of UN's Goals to achieve by the year 2030

  • By 2030, reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to less than 70 per 100,000 live births.
  • By 2030, halve the number of global deaths and injuries from road traffic accidents.
  • Strengthen the implementation of the world health organization framework convention on tobacco control in all countries, as appropiate
  • Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including narcotic drug abuse and harmful use if alcohol.
  • By 2030, substantially reduce the number of deaths and illnesses from hazardous chemicals and air, water and soil polution and contamination.
  • By 2030, end the epidemics od AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria and neglected tropical diseases and combat hepatitis, water-borne diseases and other communicable diseases.
